Who we are

AMOR DE DIOS HOPE FOUNDATION (ADDHF) is a UK/Nigerian-based registered Charity providing support and humanitarian services to the less privileged/vulnerable in the local and rural communities of Edo State,

Nigeria. Established in 2018 by Blessing Sule, ADDHF is committed to engaging in random acts of kindness, but also works at grassroots level to support and help communities for the long term.

What we do



Help for Orphans

With many Children growing up without a family to care for them, having either lost families to illness or disease, or simply because some families cannot afford to feed and clothe all their children. Many of these children end up either on the streets or in orphanages where prospects are dim. ADDHF seeks to help and support these orphans and give them not only shelter, food and clothes, but a real chance in life. This includes getting them into schools, providing medical care and also counselling therapy for vulnerable young adults.



Tackling Homelessness

ADDHF is committed to reaching out to those living on the streets or under bridges and offering them temporary shelter in the form of a sleeping tent. We work to ensure they get enough to eat and are able to receive medical attention should they need it, but we also strive to do more than that. We’d like to help them get off the streets and into permanent accommodation, so they are able to rebuild their lives.



Supporting the Elderly

There is a rising population of elderly people who have no family to care for them and for whom the system does not provide care either. For many of these vulnerable individuals, mobility issues prevent them from getting out of the house to buy food and they are also unable to cook or even feed themselves. As well as relying heavily on local foodbanks, the elderly population of Denin also benefits greatly from charitable organisations like ours that check in on them and make sure they are provided for.



Independence for Widows

Widows in the local community often have no means of supporting themselves and are left struggling to care for their families alone. ADDHF works with local organisations to help these women at a grassroots level by setting up training courses to facilitate skills-training, along with grants to start-up businesses. With the right skills, as well as mentoring, these women are given the opportunity to work and support their families. ADDHF partners with existing Charity Foundations to enable us to share collective ideas on how we can best support and help our various communities.

Our Profile

• To be a voice of hope and a light of love to the Vulnerable and needy in the society.



Mission

• Ensure no child is left alone on the streets
• Provide a home and food for orphaned children
• Provide an education and medical care for the children, as well as counselling therapy for vulnerable teens
• Support and provide provisions for the local food bank in Benin City
